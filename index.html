About me

I am currently studying randomized algorithms applied to self-organizing particle systems under Dr. Sunil Shende. The kind of research area I find myself being drawn towards involve solving theoretical problems in computer science with tangible impact on the real world. Some of my favourite courses have been in the fields of Combinatorial Optimization, Graph Theory and Probabilistic Systems Analysis.

I also work at the Senator Walter Rand Institiute for Public Affairs where I help build statistical and computational models to assist decision-making in policy. Our recent work on the shortfalls of the South Jersey healthcare system during the COVID-19 pandemic was a key resource used by local governing bodies.[impact]

Prior to joining the program, I was a Data Scientist at ParallelDots, Inc. where I worked on Bayesian machine learning methods for solving problems in market research and consumer analytics.
