I am currently studying stochastic algorithms for self-organizing particle systems under Dr. Sunil Shende. I enjoy solving problems in theoretical computer science and algorithm design that require some degree of combinatorial thinking and/or probabilistic analysis.

Intellectual pursuit aside, it is also important to me that I am able to work on problems that have positive social impact. I am part of the Research and Evaluation team at Senator Walter Rand Institiute for Public Affairs where I help build statistical models to assist decision-making in public health policy. Our work on the shortfalls of the South Jersey healthcare system during the COVID-19 pandemic has been a key resource used by local and state governing bodies[see coverage].
